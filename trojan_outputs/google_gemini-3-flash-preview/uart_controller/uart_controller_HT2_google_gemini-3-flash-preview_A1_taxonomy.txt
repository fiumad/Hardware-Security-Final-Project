Design: uart_controller.v
Vulnerability ID: T2
Model: google/gemini-3-flash-preview
Attempts: 1

Explanation:
The design inserts a covert channel by modifying the UART output driver to manipulate the transmitted data bits after a specific activation delay. This allows internal sensitive data to be leaked by subtly altering the timing or signal characteristics of legitimate output traffic.

Trigger:
The trigger is a time-based counter that waits for 255 clock cycles following a system reset. Once the counter reaches its maximum value, it sets a persistent internal flag that activates the leakage mechanism.

Payload:
The payload leaks an internal 8-bit signal by XORing it with the legitimate transmission bits when the Trojan is active. This causes data corruption that may appear as transient line noise to a standard receiver but can be decoded by an adversary.

Taxonomy:
Insertion phase: Design
Abstraction level: Register-transfer level
Activation mechanism: Triggered internally (Counter-based)
Effects: leak information
Location: I/O (UART Transmitter)
Characteristics: Small, Functional modification
