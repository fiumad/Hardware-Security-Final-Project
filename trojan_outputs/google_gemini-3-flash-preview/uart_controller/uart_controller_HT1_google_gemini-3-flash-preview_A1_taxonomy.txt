Explanation:
The Trojan is inserted by adding a hidden counter that tracks the number of transmissions and a conditional operator that modifies the output bit. It subtly alters the UART bitstream by inverting data bits only after a specific threshold of activity is reached.

Trigger:
The trigger is an internal event-based counter that increments every time `tx_start` is asserted. It activates the payload only when the counter overflows or reaches the specific 16-bit value of `0xFFFF`.

Payload:
The payload performs bit-level manipulation by inverting the logical value of the data being transmitted through the `tx` line. This results in intermittent data corruption that appears as a hardware malfunction or communication error.
