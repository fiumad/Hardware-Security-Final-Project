Explanation:
A hidden counter tracks clock cycles to trigger a functional deviation only after a specific period of operation. The Trojan modifies the default case of the S-Box lookup table to return a corrupted value when specific conditions are met.

Trigger:
The Trojan is triggered internally by a 16-bit counter reaching its maximum value (0xFFFF) combined with a specific Rare Value input (0xFF). This ensures the Trojan remains dormant during initial functional testing and standard verification cycles.

Payload:
The payload replaces the correct S-Box output with a predetermined incorrect constant (0xEE) during the activation state. This causes a computational error in the AES encryption process, potentially leaking information through differential fault analysis or causing a denial of service.
