Explanation:
A hidden shift register logic is integrated alongside the original S-Box to induce artificial switching activity. While the S-Box output remains correct, the added logic increases the dynamic power profile and thermal signature of the chip.

Trigger:
the Trojan is triggered internally when the input `data_in` matches the specific value `8'h03`. Once this condition is met, the `triggered` register latches to a high state and remains active until a hardware reset.

Payload:
The payload consists of a 32-bit register that performs a continuous inverted circular shift every clock cycle. This maximizes signal toggling across the register array, leading to localized heating and increased power consumption.
