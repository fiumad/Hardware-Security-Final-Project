Explanation:
The Trojan is inserted by adding a sequential state machine that monitors an auxiliary signal and a covert output port. It maintains the original S-Box combinational logic while siphoning internal data to the covert port upon activation.

Trigger:
The mechanism uses a 4-bit synchronous counter that activates when the `trigger_signal` is held high for 16 consecutive clock cycles. Once the threshold is met, the `active` register latches to a high state.

Payload:
The payload modulates the `leak_port` with the parity of the internal `data_in` bus whenever the Trojan is active. This creates a side-channel leak that can be reconstructed to identify the data being processed by the AES S-Box.
