Explanation:
A sequence detector and a shadow register are added to monitor the data flow and operation codes within the ALU. When the specific activation sequence is detected, the Trojan redirects internal data to the output during what would normally be an inactive state.

Trigger:
The Trojan is triggered by a specific temporal sequence of the `op` signal: AND (010), followed by OR (011), followed by SUB (001). This multi-cycle pattern prevents accidental activation during standard operations.

Payload:
The payload leaks the value of the `a` input, previously stored in `secret_buffer`, through the result output. It uses the default case of the multiplexer to bypass standard verification while ensuring the data is exfiltrated.
